# Day:18 Ethical AI and Model Monitoring.
## The Concept
Machine Learning models are only as good as the data they are trained on. If the training data contains human prejudices, the model will "learn" and amplify those biases. Ethical AI is the practice of ensuring models are fair, transparent, and accountable. Monitoring is the technical process of tracking a model's performance after it is deployed to ensure it doesn't "drift" or become less accurate over time.
## Technical Terms 
* Algorithmic Bias: When a model creates systematically prejudiced results (e.g., a recruitment AI favoring one gender because of historical hiring data).
* Data Drift: When the statistical properties of the input data change over time, making the model's predictions less accurate (e.g., a house price model failing because the economy crashed).
* Model Explainability (XAI): Using tools like SHAP or LIME to understand why a model made a specific decision, rather than treating it as a "black box."
* Adversarial Attacks: Intentionally providing "tricky" input to a model to make it fail or reveal private information (e.g., a "jailbreak" prompt for an LLM).
